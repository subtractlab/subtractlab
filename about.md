# About SubtractLab

**SubtractLab** is the research and development identity of **奥田剛司 (Koji Okuda)** — an independent builder exploring subtractive design as a first principle for AI systems.

## Who

**奥田剛司 / Koji Okuda**
Creator of [MeaningSpace](https://subtractlab.com/ARCHITECTURE), architect of [3Gravity Architecture](https://subtractlab.com/ARCHITECTURE), originator of QIS (Quantum Inspired Semantic Space).

Working independently since 2024. Building tools that subtract complexity from AI memory, not add to it.

## What

**[MeaningSpace](https://subtractlab.com/ARCHITECTURE)** — An AI-native semantic memory system in production for 2+ years with 1,400+ crystals.

Instead of scaling memory by adding more storage, MeaningSpace compresses conversations into *crystals* that carry their own evaluation — what worked, what failed, how understanding changed. Retrieval becomes reasoning: the act of remembering is itself an act of inference.

Key concepts:
- **Crystallization** — Multi-stage distillation that preserves dead ends and pivots, not just conclusions
- **3Gravity Architecture** — Crystals self-organize into clusters and galaxies by vector proximity. No folders, no labels
- **QIS (Quantum Inspired Semantic Space)** — Retrieval scored by interference: successes amplify, failures cancel

Built on DuckDB. Runs on CPU. No GPU required.

## Why "Subtract"

The AI industry scales by addition — more parameters, longer context, bigger memory stores. SubtractLab starts from the opposite premise: **AI gets smarter by remembering less, not more.**

Inspired by Leidy Klotz's research on subtraction bias (Nature, 2021) — humans systematically overlook subtractive solutions. SubtractLab applies this insight to AI system design.
